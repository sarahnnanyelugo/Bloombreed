Definition of Child Abuse and Neglect

BMS has a diverse community with multiple cultural beliefs, values and practices. To respect the global nature of the community, for the purposes of our child protection policy, the School has chosen to follow the World Health Organization’s (WHO’s) definitions of abuse and neglect.

The WHO declares that:

“Child abuse or maltreatment constitutes all forms of physical and/or emotional ill-treatment, sexual abuse, neglect, or negligent treatment, resulting in actual or potential harm to the child’s health, survival, development, or dignity in the context of a relationship of responsibility, trust, or power.”

THE PILLARS OF CHILD PROTECTION AT BMS

Our top aim is to ensure that students and the entire BMS community are safe and healthy.

BACKGROUND CHECKS

As a requirement of employment, all BMS workers must pass regular background checks.

TRAINING OF EMPLOYEES

A qualified child protection consultant trains all employees on child protection issues every three years. All new employees are trained right away.

CHILD SAFETY COMMITTEE

Our Child Safety Committee, which includes a representative from each section, ensures that any cases of child abuse or neglect receive prompt attention, investigation, and action.
